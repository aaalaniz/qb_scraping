We ran two types of clustering on the college career data. The first type of clustering is a simple two-dimensional k-means(2) clustering of a single college stat against a single nfl stat. The second type of clustering is a multi-dimensional k-means clustering of all the college career stats. The resulting clusters were mapped against the individual quarterbacks and inspected for any interesting trends against the NFL statistic variables.
